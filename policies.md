## Presentation of Course Material

In-class time will be split between lectures and fieldwork. A typical
class will consist of roughly 30-40 minutes of lecture time, during
which instructors present material in a more typical small classroom
format, followed by 70-80 minutes of practical fieldwork, where
students are led through drills and examples where they apply what
they’ve learned.

## Grading

This course, as with all 98/198 courses, must be taken on a Pass/No
Pass basis.  Grading will be based on:
- (timely) attendance (60%)
- short weekly quizzes (20%)
- weekly discussion posts (20%) 

Additionally, if you miss more than three classes, you will be
dropped. As a large part of this class is hands-on work, it’s
incredibly important that you are physically there to do it. If
extenuating circumstances arise, please contact the instructors and we
can work with you to figure something out.  All weekly quizzes are due
a week after they are posted (ie by the next class) and students will
have unlimited attempts to answer them.


## Readings & Assignments

Most weeks will have a post-class reading that students must complete
prior to the next class. Most of the readings are quite lengthy and/or
verbose, so students are not expected to read the entire
thing. Lightly skimming the reading and finding relevant material for
a discussion post is all that is required. Students will be required
to make a discussion post for each reading and respond to another
student’s discussion post to get credit for that week. Discussion
posts guidelines may vary week by week and will be listed on their
bCourses assignment page.

Readings can be found on the resources page.

In addition, there will be two tournament-style events held throughout
the semester. After each one, students will be asked to write a more
substantive reflection on their team's strategy.

While none of these assignments will be graded particularly harshly,
the instructors would like to add that **you will get out of this
course what you put in**. The more effort you put into the
assignments, the more you will learn and the more we are able to work
with you.

## Accessibility

This course involves a significant amount of physical movement, and
expects students to be able to stay on their feet throughout the
class, hold things with both hands, achieve reasonable range of motion
with both arms, and withstand light hits.

If you need to request flexibility or accommodation for a disability,
hardship, scheduling conflict, or other circumstances, please contact
the instructors as soon as possible; we will do our best to work with
you to find a solution. In addition, the Academic Accommodations Hub
has information about student rights and options. The Supportal
website can help you get connected to campus resources.
